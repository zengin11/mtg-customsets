-Hunter's Stance-
Cuz it's arcane, you can splice onto it, so it sort of enables itself. But on its own it's a dead card. Which is generally pretty bad design.

But if you've revealed one of the cards, it's slightly overcosted (these are pretty much all 1.5 mana effects). If you've revealed 2, it's efficient, and strong because of its flexibility. If you've revealed all 3 colors, it's honestly nuts as a 2 mana spell 
It says "up to one target" because if you can't provide all required targets for a spell, you can't cast it. Meaning even if you haven't revealed a white or red card, if it just said "target" you couldn't cast this spell unless you pointed at an artifact and a creature. It then wouldn't do anything to them anyway.

So, "up to one", so you can pick zero while casting and not be forced to pick stuff.
(the forced picking would problematic if there weren't any artifacts around, but you wanted the white and/or blue modes)

Plus the art's a banger

-Anguish-
Most of the time, the average mana value of most decks is around 3 when NOT considering lands—which are zero and count for around 40% of most decks and drop the real average to around 2. But it can vary significantly.

One thing I LOVE about Anguish (wild sentence to say) that I only noticed well after designing it, is that when you flashback it (cast it from your graveyard for a higher cost), it's supposed to go to exile afterwards (so you can't flashback spells over and over). However, if you hit a zero off of it, it goes to your hand instead, letting you regular cast it for 1 mana again, potentially to rinse and repeat if you're lucky.

Which feels pretty perfectly incessant for a card named Anguish.

-Huntrix's Rising Harmony-

Keep in mind that a Legendary instant means you can only play it if you control a legendary creature. So it's pretty restricted, though less so in commander, and less so again in a partner deck.

You thought thinking about splice normally was a mental exercise, gee whizz does this make it so much harder. It does NOTHING on its own, which is why it has Arcanecycling (Pay 1 mana and Discard the card from your hand to search your library for any Arcane card of your choice). So just in case you don't have any cards to splice, it works as a tutor.

But really the goal is to slam this late game, splice 2-4 cards onto it for discounts, and copy it to get triple the value.

And then, hopefully, win.

It only reduces colored costs, so something like Overblaze, which costs 2RR to splice, still costs 2R instead of just 1 mana. But splicing Overblaze is still EASILY the best option, since for 6 total mana you get to double, then double, then double the damage dealt by one creature. And Mira with 8x damage BETTER win you a game.

-Baby Saja, Ungodly Lyricist-
Baby's ability—giving pseudo-splice to all your cards—was the ability I gave to the first draft of Zoey, HUNTR/X Maknae. I removed it because it was extremely confusing; far too much so for the face commander of a precon designed for dads to get their young daughters to play magic with them.

It's very limited in three color decks, which hurts in these orecons. And it's REALLY expensive unless you have a low-cost and reliably useful card in hand. Suffers the same "man I do not have the mana budget for this" problem that Splice always does.

Something like Swords or Path is amazing to spam, though. I won a playlets game off spamming Path like five times with Baby.

A deck that's ALL cheap spells could go crazy, I suppose. If someone else builds that, I'll play against it.
